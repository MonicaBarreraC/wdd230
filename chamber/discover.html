Church of San Francisco de Asís

One of the most important and oldest former convents in the region, built in the 17th century on a pre-Hispanic temple with stones and mud, on its façade three bell towers can be seen. Founded in 1549 by Fray Luis de Villalpando, although six years later the church was dedicated to San Francisco de Asís.

Convent of the Church

As you walk through the corridors of the former convent, you will discover that they lead you to two interior patios, as well as to the cloisters and bedrooms. It is very easy to imagine the Franciscan monks in this atmosphere of silence and tranquility.

Village Festivals

There are two very popular traditional festivals, in May the festival in honor of the Virgen de la Concepción is held and at the beginning of October the festival in honor of San Francisco de Asís, the patron saint of Conkal, during these festivities it is held out vaquerías, dances, religious activities and the traditional bullfights.

Sacred Art Museum

It is the only Museum of Sacred Art in Yucatan. It has been recently remodeled and documents, sculptures, photographs, altarpieces and archives of the history of churches in the state are exhibited in 4 rooms. Here you will find a good collection of art with ecclesiastical themes, you will also be able to know the styles of several representative churches of Yucatan on a map that will locate you perfectly for your next tours.

Katoyná

Known as the ghost town or hacienda, its origin dates back to 1810 when a witch cursed the town so that the land would not be fertile and the dogs would bark at their owners. A curious fact is that it is said that a treasure is found there, in a hidden deep protected cenote.

Creole Restaurant

They are dedicated to preparing Cajun and Creole dishes. An authentic Cajun meal consists of three courses: the main course, another mainly rice, cornbread or other cereal dish and the third contains some type of vegetable.

Hacienda Santa Lucia

It has a majestic portico that welcomes us to a renewed colonial style, full of tranquility, romanticism, where time has stopped.

Cenote Cholul

The regulars of Al modar enjoy its surprising atmosphere, and for that reason they want to return again and again. This place is famous for its excellent coffee, the chef of this restaurant prepares generous sandwiches and tasty laing.

Al Modar Restaurant

The San Pedro Cholul cenote is a semi-open natural pool that allows you to swim in its fresh waters, practice snorkeling and dive in its depths of up to ten meters. In addition to its beauty, it offers its visitors basic services of toilets, showers and electricity supply. 7km from Conkal Center.
